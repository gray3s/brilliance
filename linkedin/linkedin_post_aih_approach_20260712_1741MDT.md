We have a clearer AIH direction now.

AIH is becoming a practical test framework for agentic AI hallucination,
reliability, state fidelity, and suitability to task.

The current approach has three parts:

1. Personal-history tests based on actual agentic-AI failures: provenance,
   continuity, unstable reasoning, and project-flow disturbance.

2. A K-PhD knowledge ladder: bounded exams with controlled time, I/O, grading,
   and reference-source rules such as Wikipedia-only.

3. AI Chess Match: a formal state-fidelity smoke test where agents must make
   legal moves, preserve board state, operate under timing rules, and reach a
   classified game ending.

The point is not to claim that one AI stack is broadly "better." The point is
to compare HybridAI, Codex, local models, cloud agents, and future agentic
stacks under the same bounded tests.
