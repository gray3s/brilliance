# Brilliance v1 Direction

Created: 2026-07-12

## Decision

Further consideration of the three initial cash-reward / prize-problem
families will be treated as `v1`.

The three families are:

1. Clay Millennium Prize Problems.
2. XPRIZE-style challenges.
3. Other formally stated prize or cash-reward problems with clear validation
   criteria.

## v1 Goal

Evaluate which family is the best first case study for the Brilliance project.

The evaluation should consider:

- clarity of problem statement,
- availability of official rules,
- validation criteria,
- cash reward or prize structure,
- likelihood of AI hallucination,
- usefulness as a bounded AI-analysis test,
- feasibility of human-assisted decomposition,
- risk of overclaiming,
- public readability.

## v1a-c Branch Plan

After v1 evaluates the three families, develop separate branches:

- `v1a`: Clay Millennium Prize Problem case study.
- `v1b`: XPRIZE-style challenge case study.
- `v1c`: Other cash-reward / prize-problem case study.

Each branch should include:

- official problem statement or rules link,
- AI initial attempt,
- waffle/overclaim analysis,
- human assistance,
- revised AI attempt,
- evidence and uncertainty log,
- next testable subproblem.

## Current Status

No v1 case study has been selected yet. The next task is to choose the first
candidate problem family and identify a specific problem within it.
